# PRD.md — Fab Footprint

## 1. Summary

**Fab Footprint** is an interactive web app that reveals the hidden environmental cost of manufacturing a semiconductor chip. A user picks a familiar chip (Apple A18, NVIDIA H100, an HBM3 stack, a generic 3nm die), and the app shows water consumed, energy used, GHG emissions, and PFAS / fluorinated-gas emissions associated with making a single unit — with every number backed by a cited source. Google Gemini then generates a plain-English explanation and answers follow-up questions, grounded in the same data.

**Submission target:** DEV Weekend Challenge: Earth Day Edition (theme: *Build for the Planet*), due April 20, 2026, 12:29 PM IST. Primary prize category: **Best Use of Google Gemini**.

---

## 2. Problem

The semiconductor industry's environmental footprint is real, large, and almost invisible to the public. Most sustainability conversations focus on the device's *use phase* (the phone battery, the data center PUE). Very little attention is paid to the *manufacturing phase*, where a single 300mm wafer can consume ~8,000 liters of ultra-pure water, tens of kWh of energy, and release fluorinated gases with GWPs thousands of times higher than CO₂.

At the same time, "AI" is now a household word, and people are asking what it costs the planet. They Google "how much water does a GPU use" and get a wall of contradictory blog posts. There is no approachable, honest, cited-from-primary-sources tool that lets a non-expert see the numbers for a specific chip.

---

## 3. Goal & non-goals

### Goals
- Make a non-expert *viscerally understand* the manufacturing footprint of a chip in under 90 seconds.
- Ground every number in a cited, linkable source.
- Demonstrate meaningful, non-gimmicky use of Google Gemini (grounded explanation + Q&A).
- Ship by Monday April 20, 12:29 IST.

### Non-goals
- Full life-cycle assessment (LCA) rigor. We're an illustrative tool, not a research-grade LCA.
- Covering the use phase (that's well-covered elsewhere).
- Comparing specific chipmakers to shame any of them. We show industry physics.
- User accounts, saved state, social features.
- Real-time data from fabs. Static curated data is fine for v1.

## 4. Target users

- **Primary:** DEV Weekend Challenge judges — technical, skim-reading, looking for relevance-to-theme + creativity + technical execution + writing quality.
- **Secondary:** engineers and hobbyists curious about the AI-vs-planet conversation, who'll share the tool on Bluesky / LinkedIn / Hacker News.
- **Tertiary:** journalists and educators looking for a citable visualization.

The common trait: moderate-to-high technical literacy, short attention span, skeptical of hand-wavy numbers.

---

## 5. User stories

1. **As a judge**, I land on the page and in 5 seconds understand what this is and why it exists.
2. **As a curious engineer**, I pick a chip I recognize (an A18, an H100) and immediately see water / energy / GHG numbers for making one.
3. **As a skeptic**, I click any number and see the source it came from.
4. **As a non-expert**, I read a 2–3 paragraph AI-generated explanation that makes the numbers feel real ("this is like X bathtubs of water").
5. **As a curious user**, I type "why does EUV use so much energy?" into a chat box and get a grounded, factual answer that references the data on screen.
6. **As a share-er**, I copy a URL that loads the same chip I was looking at (deep-linkable state).

## 6. Functional requirements

### F1. Chip selection
- A landing screen shows 6–8 chip cards (preset list from `data/chips.json`).
- Each card shows: chip name, vendor, process node, launch year, one-line "what it powers."
- Cards are keyboard-navigable and have hover state.
- Clicking a card transitions to the dashboard view.

### F2. Footprint dashboard
Once a chip is selected, show:

- **Header:** chip name, node, die size, estimated mask count.
- **Four primary metric tiles:** Water (L), Energy (kWh), GHG (kg CO₂e), PFAS/F-gas (kg CO₂e equivalent from fluorinated process gases). Each tile shows: number, unit, short human comparison ("= X bathtubs", "= X AC-hours"), and confidence badge.
- **Process breakdown chart:** horizontal bar chart, Recharts, showing each major process family's contribution (lithography, etch, deposition, CMP, cleans, implant, metrology) to each metric. Toggle which metric drives the bar lengths.
- **Citations drawer:** clicking any number opens a side sheet listing the sources backing it, each linkable.

### F3. Gemini-powered explanation
- Below the dashboard, a "Why these numbers?" section auto-streams a 2–3 paragraph explanation generated by Gemini. The prompt includes the full chip+process data as grounding context.
- On first load of a chip, this call fires automatically. Result cached per chip ID in memory for the session.
- A "regenerate" button exists.

### F4. Grounded Q&A
- A persistent chat input lets the user ask anything about the current chip.
- Every call sends the current chip's data + the conversation history.
- Streaming response.
- System instruction tells Gemini to answer only from the provided data and to say "I don't have that data" when asked about things outside it.

### F5. Deep-linkable state
- URL encodes the selected chip ID: `/?chip=a18`.
- Sharing this URL opens the same chip view.

### F6. About / methodology modal
- One link in the footer opens a modal explaining: what the numbers mean, what they don't mean, how we sourced them, known limitations.

## 9. Out of scope (for v1)

- Custom chip builder (user enters node + die size).
- Packaging-stage footprint (CoWoS, HBM stacking) — we focus on front-end-of-line + middle-of-line per wafer, amortized to one die.
- Upstream mining / material extraction.
- Use-phase energy.
- Chipmaker-specific "scorecards."
- Internationalization.
- Authentication.
- Analytics.

## 10. Open questions / assumptions

- **Assumption:** average-industry numbers from TSMC / IMEC / SEMI publications are representative enough for public education. Acceptable for a weekend build; we disclose in the methodology modal.
- **Assumption:** per-wafer numbers can be amortized to per-die by (die area / usable wafer area) × (1 / yield). We'll use published yield estimates where available, else 70% as a reasonable default with a note.
- **Open:** whether to show PFAS as a separate metric or fold it into GHG via GWP. **Decision:** show both — raw PFAS (kg) and its CO₂e contribution folded into the GHG tile — because PFAS has a persistence story the CO₂e number hides.
